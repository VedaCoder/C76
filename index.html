Introduction to Black Holes

What is a Black Hole?

A black hole is a place in space where gravity is super strong and nothing can escape from it. It's like a vacuum cleaner that sucks everything in, even light. Scientists think that when a really big star dies, it becomes a black hole.

Types of Black Holes

There are three types of black holes:

  • Stellar black holes: These are the smallest kind of black holes and are made when a really big star dies.
  • Intermediate black holes: These are bigger than stellar black holes but smaller than supermassive black holes. Scientists are not sure how they are formed.
  • Supermassive black holes: These are the biggest kind of black holes and are found in the center of galaxies. Scientists think they are formed by merging smaller black holes.

Interesting Facts about Black Holes

Here are some cool things to know about black holes:

  • Black holes are not actually black. They give off radiation called Hawking radiation.
  • The first black hole ever discovered was in 1964.
  • If you got too close to a black hole, you would get stretched out like spaghetti. Eww!
